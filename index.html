How a Search Engine Thinks

A search engine is a system that crawls the web, indexes pages, and ranks them to answer our questions in milliseconds.

Crawlers Index Ranking algorithms Ads
1
Crawl
Bots follow links and download pages from across the web.
➜
2
Index
Content is cleaned, analysed and stored in a massive, searchable index.
➜
3
Rank
Algorithms score pages using relevance, freshness and authority signals.
➜
4
Respond & Learn
Results, clicks and dwell time become new data to improve the next search.
Future insight: Over the next decade, large language models will turn search into conversation, returning answers instead of lists of links — but this makes transparency and source attribution much more difficult.

How Big Data Powers It

Every query, click and scroll becomes part of big data: huge, messy datasets that machine learning models use to spot patterns and make predictions.

Volume Variety Velocity Veracity
Collect
Search logs, location, clicks, devices, and behaviour streams.
Store
Distributed databases and cloud warehouses keep petabytes of data online.
Analyse
ML models learn to rank, recommend, and detect anomalies.
Act
Personalised results, ads and predictions are delivered in real time.
Critical view: Big data makes search more accurate and personalised, but it also raises questions of privacy, bias and energy use in huge data centres. Future regulation and greener infrastructure will be crucial.
